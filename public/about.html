What is a high-altitude balloon?


Helium weather balloon that can carry a small payload. Payload can include sensors, cameras, experiments.

Ascends to over 100,000 ft, well into the stratosphere.

Balloon expands as it rises and eventually pops.

Payload returns to Earth with a parachute.

Data capture and launch vehicle development


Programming on Arduinos

Students will form groups and have a few weeks to develop data logging code

11 sensors per flight, forming groups to work on each sensor

Everyone is encouraged to participate, even those without Arduino experience

Data analyses

Everyone will be involved in data analyses. Project ideas include but are not limited to:

  1. Atmospheric layers: Determine where the tropopause is and map the change in temperature.
  2. Changes over time: With 2 launches, see how measurements change in different seasons. Average monthly CO2 changes significantly throughout the year.
  3. Measuring gravity: Calculate the change in gravitational force as altitude increases, accounting for the acceleration of the balloon and noise in the signal
  4. Measure earth circumference based on photos
  5. Compare GPS altimeter to Barometric Altimeter
